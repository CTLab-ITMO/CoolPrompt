Key Findings

  • PE2+SGR is the best-performing method overall, achieving an average improvement of +0.0988 across 43 matched tasks (vs PE2 +0.0825, APE +0.0118, OPRO +0.0064)
  • PE2+SGR wins 27/43 tasks (63%), PE2 wins 10, APE wins 3, OPRO wins 3
  • 12 tasks with strong SGR lead (>0.02 over next best method), vs only 1 tasks with significant loss (>0.05)
  • PE2+SGR leads in every category: BBH, Instruction Induction, and Counterfactual Evaluation
  • Head-to-head vs PE2 (54 tasks): PE2+SGR wins 26, PE2 wins 14, ties 14 — SGR avg +0.1112 vs PE2 avg +0.1005

Methodology

Model: gpt-4o-mini (OpenAI)

Optimization: Beam search with 3 training steps, beam width 3, 4 expansions per node, batch size 4

Evaluation: BERTScore for text tasks, Exact Match for numeric tasks. 100 samples per task, 75/25 train/val split.

PE2+SGR Innovation: Adaptive dual-path proposer. Starts with free-form reasoning (enhanced with cross-example pattern analysis) for radical prompt exploration. Automatically switches to structured guided reasoning (SGR) with Pydantic-validated diagnosis when improvement plateaus (delta < 0.02), enabling precision refinement. Strategy override prevents conservatism bias in structured mode.
